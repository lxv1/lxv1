The Ramsey theorem and the Waring problem for algebras over fields

 

L. N. Vaserstein

 

                        Abstract. We show that for each integer k ³ 1 there is an integer   R(k)   such that every element of every algebra  A  over every  finite field F = kF   is a sum or difference of k  k-th powers as well as the sum of  3k/2  k-th powers provided that card(F)  ³ R(k). Moreover, the statement on sum or differences holds also for any infinite field  F = kF  such that the subgroup of the k-th powers in the multiplicative group  F* is of a finite index.

 

0. Statement of results

 

                  For any integer k ³ 1 and any  ring  A  we denote by  ±Ak the set of all  ±ak  with  a Î A. Following [6], we denote by  Ak  the  additive subgroup of  A  generated by ±Ak,  and we denote by vk(A)  the least  integer s ³ 0  such that every  element of  Ak is a sum or difference of  s  k-th powers.   We denote by wk(A) the least  integer s ³ 0  such that every sum of k-th powers in  A is the sum of  s  k-th powers.  Note that vk(A)= wk(A) when -1 is a k-th power in the center  C of A, e.g., when  k  is odd. 

                  Assume now  that  the center   C  of  A  contains a field  F. A few upper bounds are given in [6]  for the numbers vk(A) and wk(A). For example, by Theorem 3(d) of [6], vk(A) ² wk(A) ² k(k+1)/2 when  card(F) ³ k2 – k +1 and  vk(A) ² wk(A) ² 2k – 1 when  card(F) ³ k4, provided that  F is finite and  either  kA ­ 0 or  A = C. In this paper we prove that vk(A) ² k  and   wk(A)  ² 3k/2  for sufficiently large card(F), provided that  F is finite and  either  kA ­ 0 or  A = C. In fact we obtain a more precise result. Namely, we replace  the upper bound  k by a number  k(p)   ² k where    p  is the characteristic of the field  F.

                  We set   k(p)   = ¸(ai + 1)  – 1, where ai  are the digits of  k  in base  p (so  k = ·ai pi). It is clear that  k(p)    ² k and that k(p)    = k   if and only if  1 ² k ² p – 1 or  k = pm – 1 for some integer  m ³ 1.

                  (iii) there is an integer  R(k)  depending only on  k, such that  vk(A) ² k(p)   ²  k and   wk(A) ² 3 k(p) /2  ² 3k/2    when q ³ R(k).
