4. An American Roulette coupon pays $10 if you win. It requires you to bet at least $100 of your money

 with the coupon.  You can bet on red or black. The value of the coupon is  (A) -$10/19, (B),$1, (C) $40/19.


Solution. If you bet  b≥ 100 with the coupon, your payment is  b + 10 with probability  9/19 and -b with probability 10/19. 

The expected payoff is  90/19 - b/19. It reaches the maximal value  -10/19 at b = 100. Since the value of game is negative, it is better to avoid it if possible.

A.

6.In Blackjack, you have 20 hard. The dealer shows 10. Remaining cards are  10,10,10,9,9.  Your bet is $10. 

The value of game is (A) -$1, (B)  0, (C) $4, (D) $5.


Solution.  If you draw, you lose, so you stand at 20.

The hole card is  10 with probability 3/5 (in which case you payoff is 0) or 9 with probability 2/5 (in which case your payoff is $10).

 Your expected payoff is $10*2/5 = $4.

C.

If you have 20 soft instead of 20 hard, you have to consider drawing.

With probability 3/5, you  get   10  and go to 20 hard. Then you must stand. Your expected payoff is 1/2 *0 + 1/2 *$109 = $5.

With probability 2/5, you  get   9  and go  to 19 hard. Then you must stand. Your expected payoff is 3/4 *(-$10) + 1/4 *0 =-$7.5.

So if you draw, your expected payoff is  3/5 * $5 +  2/5 * (-$7.5) =  0. It is not optimal.

Therefore you should stand at 20 soft and  the value of game is $4.
