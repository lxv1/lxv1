27.  The arbitration pair   for   game in #26 is 

(A) (1, 1),    (B) (3, 3), (C)  (41/12, 43/14), (D)  (3.5, 3.5).



Solution.  The mixtures of the  given 4 pure joint payoffs form a triangle with (0, 6), (7, 0), and (1,1)   as the corners. The point (3, 3) is inside the triangle.It is not Pareto optimal among the mixed joint payoffs.

The Pareto optimal solutions are the mixtures of  (0, 6) and  (7, 0) which is  the side of the triangle with negative slope.

The straight line containing  this side   is 6x + 7y = 42.

The initial point  (x0, y0) for Nash bargaining  is (1,1), see #22.

So we maximize  (x - 1)(y - 1)  on the line  6x + 7y = 42.


Since 6(x - 1) +7 (y - 1) = 29, the maximum is reached at  6(x - 1) =  7(y - 1) =  29/2 = 14.5, , hence

x =  20.5/6  =  41/12  y = 21.5/7 = 43/14 .

Since   0 ≤  41/12 ≤  7, the point  (x, y)  = (41/12, 43/14)  belongs to the Pareto  optimal side.

Since  (x0, yo) = (1,1) ≤  (41/12, 43/14), we found the arbitration pair

(x*, y*) =  (41/12, 43/14).

Answer,  (C).



28. The Shapley values   for  game in #26  are
