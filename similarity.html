 U(A -  λ)V = B -  λ. Then  A  and  B  are similar. 

Proof.  U = (B -  λ)Q_1 + R_1   V = Q_2(B -  λ) + R_2,                                                      

R_1(A -  λ)R_2 = (U - (B -  λ)Q_1) (A -  λ) (V - Q_2(B -  λ)  =   (B -  λ) + (B -  λ)(*)(B -  λ)

where  * =    Q_1 (A -  λ)Q_2  -  Q_1) /U  - Q_2 - (1/V)Q_2.

The second term on the right vanishes because otherwise  it is of degree  ≥  2.

Then R_1 R_2 = 1 and   R_1AR_2 = B.

DeMeyer, Frank(1-COS); Miranda, Rick(1-COS); Schneider, Kim(1-COS)

Examples of the failure of the local-global principle for similarity of matrices. (English summary) 

Comm. Algebra 26 (1998), no. 12, 4179–4185. 

13C99 (15A21) . Miranda.pdf

Let R be a commutative ring and M and N finitely presented R-modules. Say that f∈EndR(M) and g∈EndR(N) are similar if αf=gα for some isomorphism α from M to N. Similarly, one defines f and g to be locally similar if there is a local isomorphism αP for each maximal ideal (or prime ideal) P of R intertwining f and g (and so necessarily M and N are locally isomorphic). Let S=R[x]/I, where I is the ideal of R[x] which annihilates f. Then letting x act on M as f induces an S-module structure on M. The study of endomorphisms locally similar to f is precisely the study of S-modules locally isomorphic to M. In particular, one may bring to bear the various tools of module theory and cohomology. In particular, if R is a 1-dimensional Noetherian ring, then so is S and the Mayer-Vietoris sequence can be used to study this problem. Also, under mild hypotheses, one can impose a natural abelian group structure on the set of local similarity classes. In this paper, this theory is reviewed and various examples of such situations are discussed.

Reviewed by Robert M. Guralnick

 MR0766270 (86c:16003)  Estes, Dennis R.(1-SCA); Guralnick, Robert M.(1-SCA)

Representations under ring extensions: Latimer-MacDuffee and Taussky correspondences.

Adv. in Math. 54 (1984), no. 3, 302–313.

16A18 (16A64)

This article presents generalizations of the classical Latimer-MacDuffee and Taussky correspondences establishing a bijection between similarity classes of nonsingular integral n×n matrices with a common separable characteristic polynomial f(x) and the classes of faithful ideals in Z[x]/(f(x)).

   Let R be an arbitrary commutative ring and let A be a rank n projective R-algebra. The n×n matrix classes are replaced by the isomorphism classes in ℳA, the category of rank n projective R-modules which are faithful right A-modules. Let S be a commutative R-algebra and let ℳA(S) [resp. IA(S)] denote the category of those M∈ℳA becoming trivial under scalar extension by S, that is, S⊗RM≅S⊗RA [resp. the isomorphism classes of full fractional ideals in S⊗RA]. R⊂S implies that the isomorphism classes of ℳA(S) and IS(A) coincide. The full Latimer-MacDuffee correspondence is then obtained if ℳA=ℳA(S). Various situations where this happens are studied. For instance if A is commutative and S is a flat R-algebra with a certain localization property without proper finitely generated faithful ideals, then ℳA=ℳA(S) if and only if HomR(A,R)∈ℳA(S). Moreover, ℳA=ℳA(S) for some overring S of R if and only if ℳA=ℳA(R𝔭) for all localizations of R at prime ideals 𝔭 which do not contain any finitely generated faithful ideals. The results apply to partly classical cases considered by Noether-Deuring, Reiner-Zassenhaus, the authors, and Roggenkamp. Finally, Taussky's dual correspondence is generalized, and also eigenmodules are considered in order to clarify Taussky's correspondence in the more general situation.

Reviewed by Alfred G. Wiedemann

 Let $R$ be a principal ideal local ring of length two, for example, the ring $R=\Z/p^2\Z$ with $p$ prime. In this paper we develop a theory of normal forms for similarity classes in the matrix rings $M_n(R)$ by interpreting them in terms of extensions of $R[t]$-modules. Using this theory, we describe the similarity classes in $M_n(R)$ for $n\leq 4$, along with their centralizers. Among these, we characterize those classes which are similar to their transposes. Non-self-transpose classes are shown to exist for all $n>3$. When $R$ has finite residue field of order $q$, we enumerate the similarity classes and the cardinalities of their centralizers as polynomials in $q$. Surprisingly, these polynomials turn out to have non-negative integer coefficients.

Similarity and commutators of matrices over principal ideal rings

Alexander Stasinski Comments: 23 pages; minor corrections

We prove that if R is a principal ideal ring and A\in\M_n(R) is a matrix with trace zero, then A is a commutator, that is, A=XY-YX for some X,Y\in\M_n(R). This generalises the corresponding result over fields due to Albert and Muckenhoupt, as well as that over Z due to Laffey and Reams, and as a by-product we obtain new simplified proofs of these results. We also establish a normal form for similarity classes of matrices over PIDs, generalising a result of Laffey and Reams. This normal form is a main ingredient in the proof of the result on commutators.

integer

 Given a principal ideal domain R and a fixed integer n the author considers four categories. These are: C1, the category of square matrices T over R with Tn=0; C2, the category of finitely generated R-free R[X]/(Xn)-modules; C3, the category of n-step filtrations F0⊃F1⊃⋯⊃Fn=0 of R-modules with F0 finitely generated and free; and C4, the category of (n−1)-step filtrations G0⊃G1⊃⋯⊃Gn−1=0 of R-modules, with G0 finitely generated. In each case the morphisms are the isomorphisms in the obvious sense. After pointing out some functors between these categories, the author concentrates on C3 and C4. His main interest is in determining when objects in these categories are naively determined, i.e., determined up to isomorphism by the factors of the filtrations. Several sufficient conditions are given for the case where n≤3. As an application, he obtains a canonical form for square R-matrices with cube zero.
